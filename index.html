So what are some aspects of Interface Design and Web Design? Interface Design is engaged in the creation, improvement, and features of visual systems for an “End User” (you or another person), and technology converges to communicate via inputs and outputs. We interact with a plethora of digital interfaces on a daily basis. A few examples of these visual systems are in our hand-held devices commonly referred to as applications or “apps”. One of the most current terms in the design world today is bundled as UX/UI designer. A UX/UI designer specializes in both Interface Design and User Experience Design.
It would NOT be an understatement to say that life as we know it would be chaotic without the seamless yet pervasive nature of UX/UI design. Everything ranging from entertainment to transportation, commerce, health, education, and lifestyle is defined via UX/UI. Interface Designers are invested in understanding and developing the features of visual systems that facilitate the “Affordances” (The aspects that resolve or engage end-users needs) versus the “Pain-Points”. Pain points are areas of design systems that are lacking or come short when addressing the end-users needs.
