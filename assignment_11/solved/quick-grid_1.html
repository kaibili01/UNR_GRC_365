Example #2

Prior to Boostrap 5 you were required to specify how big a column was. But this can be difficult if you do not know the size of the itemn you want to integrate into a column. Boostrap 5 and up has a default setting were if column sizes are not specified, then all columns divide space equally. Or if some columns specify the amount of space to be used, and other's do not, then the remaining space available is allocated to the remaining column(s). This is known as "auto-layout-columns"

When you are not sure of the content size that you will be using. Then you can rely on the 'auto' feature. This can result in content being flushed to the left. We can resolve that issue by adding the a class in to the row such as 'justify-content-md-center'. In this case content will stay centered as long as the view port is equal or less than ≥768px.
